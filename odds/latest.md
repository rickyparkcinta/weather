## Risks and Invalidation Factors

- Public Polymarket pages can lag executable order books, especially in thin weather buckets.
- US temperature markets resolve on Wunderground station history, not directly on NWS forecasts or NWS observation tables.
- Austin is now a live-station/high-so-far problem: 80-81F, 82-83F, and 84-85F all remain plausible depending on whether storms persist or breaks in precipitation allow renewed heating.
- Houston and Dallas are storm-timing markets. Morning or early-afternoon convection can cap highs one or two buckets below the NWS daily high, while later clearing can still push warmer.
- HKO is a one-tenth-degree boundary market. A 30.0C official maximum invalidates 29C YES and 30C NO even though 29.9C has held so far.
- Chicago and Atlanta are narrow exact-bucket setups where one-degree rounding, timing of cloud cover, or brief warm pushes can flip the result.
